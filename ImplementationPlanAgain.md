Real-Life Government Procurement: Start to Finish
🟦 PHASE 0 — Before Anything Gets Bought (Planning)
This is the part most people don't see. Before a single peso is spent:
Annual Procurement Plan (APP)
Every department (e.g., health office, engineering office) lists down everything they plan to buy for the entire year — what it is, how much it costs (the ABC), and when they plan to buy it. These are consolidated by the BAC Secretariat, certified by the Budget Officer, and approved by the HOPE (the Mayor or head of the agency). This becomes the approved APP — a public document.

🟦 PHASE 1 — Someone Needs Something (Purchase Requisition)
When a department actually needs to buy something:

The Department Requester (e.g., the Municipal Health Officer) creates a Purchase Requisition (PR) — essentially a formal internal request saying "we need X items at Y cost."
The Department Head endorses it.
The Budget Officer certifies that funds are actually available.
The BAC Secretariat receives it, checks if it aligns with the APP, and assigns the appropriate procurement mode based on the ABC amount.

🟦 PHASE 3 — Public Advertisement (for Competitive Bidding)
The BAC Secretariat prepares an Invitation to Bid (ITB), reviewed and signed by the BAC Chairperson, then posted on:

PhilGEPS (mandatory)
The agency's website
Physical bulletin board

The law enforces minimum advertisement periods — the public must have enough time to see and respond to the invitation before bids are accepted.

🟦 PHASE 4 — Vendors Respond
Interested suppliers:

Purchase the bidding documents
Attend the pre-bid conference (if required)
Prepare and submit their bid in two sealed envelopes: one for eligibility/technical documents, one for the financial bid (price)

In real life this is entirely physical — sealed envelopes submitted before the deadline.

⚠️ One nuance: In your ProcureSeal flow, vendor onboarding is done by the BAC Secretariat registering the bidder manually. In real life, vendors register themselves on PhilGEPS first, then show up with physical documents. ProcureSeal adapts this to a controlled digital environment, which is a reasonable design choice.


🟦 PHASE 5 — Bid Opening
On the scheduled date, the BAC opens bids publicly:

BAC must have a quorum (minimum 3 members)
COA/GPPB observers may be present
First envelope (eligibility documents) is opened and evaluated — Pass or Fail per document
Only bidders who pass eligibility have their second envelope (financial/price bid) opened
Results are recorded in the Abstract of Bids As Read


✅ Your flow reflects this well. The quorum confirmation and pass/fail recording are accurate.


🟦 PHASE 6 — Evaluation
The BAC and TWG:

Review technical compliance of eligible bidders
Apply arithmetic corrections to the bid prices if needed → this gives the Calculated Price
Rank bidders from lowest to highest calculated price
The lowest becomes the Lowest Calculated Bid (LCB)

The BAC must decide whether to use LCRB (lowest calculated responsive bid), MEARB (most economically advantageous responsive bid), MARB, or HRRB as the criteria for contract award Lexology — this is a new feature under RA 12009 that didn't exist before.

🟦 PHASE 7 — Post-Qualification
The Rank 1 / LCB is not automatically awarded the contract. The BAC still needs to verify:

Are their submitted documents authentic?
Do they have the financial capacity to perform the contract?
Do they have the technical capability (equipment, personnel, track record)?

The bidder is given 5 calendar days to submit post-qualification documents. If they fail, the BAC moves to Rank 2 and repeats.

🟦 PHASE 8 — Award
Once the BAC confirms the LCRB (Lowest Calculated Responsive Bid):

BAC drafts a BAC Resolution recommending award
HOPE reviews and approves
Notice of Award (NOA) is issued to the winning bidder
Bidder must acknowledge receipt within 3 calendar days
Bidder submits Performance Security (a bond, usually 2–10% of contract amount)
Contract is signed
Notice to Proceed (NTP) is issued → this is Day 1 of implementation


✅ Your understanding here is accurate. The NOA acknowledgment and NTP as the start of the clock are both correct.


🟦 PHASE 9 — Contract Implementation & Payment

Supplier delivers the goods or completes the work
Department Requester and inspection team inspect the delivery
Inspection and Acceptance Report (IAR) is signed
Supplier submits an invoice
Finance Officer does a three-way match: Invoice vs. Purchase Order vs. IAR
Payment is processed
